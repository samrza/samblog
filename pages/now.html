Now

a 22-year-old computer science student who spends most of his time between hostel life, code, and long thoughts about the world. I’m not here to pretend I have it all figured out — I’m still learning, still trying, and still messing up. But I believe in movement — slow, stubborn progress.

I’m currently studying in a CS engineering program and trying to build things that are useful, simple, and maybe a bit meaningful. That includes websites, travel tools, and a SaaS.

I'm not chasing “hustle” life, but I do believe in consistency, clarity, and quiet confidence. I write sometimes, mostly for myself — to understand, not to impress. I build with what I have — a minimal budget, a laptop, and a head full of ideas I’m trying to turn real. This blog isn’t a polished portfolio. It’s a trail map. You’ll find half-finished thoughts, side projects, small experiments, and some honest notes on trying to become a better climber — in tech, in life, and sometimes literally (I’ve started mountain trekking too).

If you’re curious what I’m currently working on, thinking about, or exploring, you can check out my Now Page
