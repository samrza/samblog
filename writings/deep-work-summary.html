Deep Work by Cal Newport

Part 1: The Idea

deep work = rare , hard to replicate
shallow work = non demanding , easy replicable
deep life is not just economically lucarative but also life well lived

1. Deep Work is Valuable

Deep work reduces attention residue and leads to higher performance
streamlines attention and increase happiness ( neurological )
generates flow that satisfies our life ( flow - psychological )
mindset of craftmanship ( builder )
those who will thrive are - high skilled worker, superstars, owners

four pillar of deep work :
1. two core abilities of thriving
a. ability to quickly master hard things
b. ability to produce at an elite level in terms of both quality and speed.
quality determines opportunity time and speed determines competition out.

how deep work enables mastery :
deliberate practice
high quality work produced = time spent x intensity of focus

problem - attention residue
soln - work on single task for long period without switching

2. Deep Work is Rare

shallow work is easier - so mass prefer
a. the principle of least resistance - we naturally gravitate towards what easier at that moment.
b. busyness as proxy for productivity
c. culture of connectivity

3. Deep Work is Meaningful

if you read flow by Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi then you know why deep work is valuable
our brain construct our worldview based on what we pay attention to.
deep work focus your attention on thing that matter creating richer mental world.

Part 2: The Rules (How to do Deep Work)

there are 4 ways you can incorporate deep work in your life:
Monastic: Eliminate almost all shallow work and stay permanently disconnected to focus deeply.
Bimodal: Alternate between long stretches of deep isolation and periods of normal, connected work.
Rhythmic: Do deep work at the same time every day to make it a fixed habit.
Journalistic: Switch into deep work mode whenever time appears, without a fixed schedule.

i prefer rythmic bcz this fits my lifestyle, but most of you also use this.

Rule#3: Quit Social Media

###rules 3 - quit social media
problem - any-benefit mindset
soln : craftsmanship approach to tool selection

process :
1. identify your main high level goal
professional goal (2-3)
personal goal ( 2-3)
2. identify 2-3 most important activities that help you achieve goal.
3. assess each tool
Q what positive impact does it have on these on these key activities ?

4. keep if the positive outweigh negative, otherwise quit.

##THE LAW OF VITAL FEW ( 80/20 rule)
out of 10-15 supporting your goal, only 2-3 actually make significant difference, other literally steal time.

30 day social media ban challenge
to break fomo:
1. ban yourself from all social media for 30 days.
2. don’t anounce you are doing this.
3. don’t formally delete.

after 30 days ask 2 question :
a) would the last 30 days have been notably better if i used this service?
b) did people care that i wasn’t using this service.

decision rules :
both answer “no” - quit permanently
both answer “yes” - resume again
a month without it reveals how little actually miss
